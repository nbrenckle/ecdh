Diffie-Hellman


Alice and Bob will now exchange their A and B values with each other. A and B are also public information, and will be known by both parties and anyone who might listen. The values of α and β are kept secret, known only to Alice and Bob, repsectively. Using the public A and B values, along with their private α and β values, Alice and Bob can each calculate the shared point P. Point P can be computed as α * B or β * A. This will produce the same result for either user. Now both Alice and Bob have a shared private key they can use to exchange information. Anyone listening in could not calculate the shared point P or derive their secret points α or β. This process, calculating a shared secret using one bit of secret information and one bit of public information is the 'Diffie-Hellman' part of the process.
