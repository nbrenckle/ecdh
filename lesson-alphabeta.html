We have completed calculating the domain paramaters! The domain parameters consist of the equation values a, b and Z from the first page, along with the generator point G, the order n and the cofactor h from the second page. It is now time to select Alice and Bob's private keys. Public key encyption employs what is known as a 'trapdoor' function. A trapdoor function is one that is very easy to solve in one direction, but very hard to work backwards to find factors from the solution. While the generator point G is known to all parties, and all parties will know the result of α * G and β * G, trying to calculate the values of α and β from αG and βG is very difficult. This is also called the Elliptic Curve discrete logarithm problem. α and β are values between 1 and the order, or with our choices, between 1 and 000. Enter a value for α, Alices private key.

Press Calculate for Bob to choose a random β value for Bob. While Alice and Bob can choose the same random value, choose different ones your first time through. Alice and Bob have their keys selected, press Next to move onto the next page.
