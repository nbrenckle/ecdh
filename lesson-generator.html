Above you can see all the points on your curve generated from the formula. On the left is a scatter plot of all the points, and on the right is a text listing. Alice and Bob must now select their point 'G', the generator point. From this one point we can build a set of points, G, 2G, 3G, 4G, until we reach an infinity point. Select one of the points on the curve and enter it in the box below. Then press Calculate. The number of points generated in this set will be called the order, and designated with the letter 'n'. We will also calculate the co-factor, designated with the letter 'h'. The cofactor is determined by the number of points on the elliptic curve divided by the order. An ideal cofactor is 1. An ellptic curve and generator point with a cofactor greater than 4 is more susceptible to attack, and is not a good choice. For this lesson you can be less concerned with choosing an ideal cofactor and simply choose one that gives you a number of elements to work with. If you used the suggested values from the other page, point 87,10 makes for a good generator point. If you cannot find a suitable G point to work with, you should select 'Reset' on the left of the page and choose new values to generate your curve.
